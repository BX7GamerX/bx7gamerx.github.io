For Ivy

A poem for you, Ivy, in this quiet moment.

In the hollow where your laughter once bloomed bright,

Time stretched like dusk, fading slow into night.

But now, freed from the chains of last week's grind,

Let’s steal back hours we lost to the bind.

Come, let’s stitch the day with threads of gold and fire,

Each moment alive, burning wild with desire—

No rush, just us, where breath and laughter meet,

In tangled hours, both tender and sweet.

With every line, I hope you know I'm here for you.
